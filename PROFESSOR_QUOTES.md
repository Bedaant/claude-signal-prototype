## Creativity of Solution: 3 Levels

> "The way we look at creativity is from 3 different levels."

**Level 1: Does it actually solve the problem?**
> "The first level: Is that solution even addressing the given problem or the identified problem or not? Sounds very obvious, right? But believe me, a lot of the candidates come up with solutions which does not even map to the problem that you've identified."

> "Problem is something, solution is something else. Which is basically showcasing what we started off this fellowship with: do not be solution-oriented."

> "The solutions that they come up with might sound very fancy, but it will not meet the problem statement that you've identified, and that's your first failure."

**Level 2: Is it differentiated?**
> "The second level: Is the solution that has been thought of differentiated? Is it different from what is existing in the market? That's the second thing. Because only then the solution will be treated as novel and unique."

> "If you're doing the same thing which is already there in the market, it's a solution, it is addressing the problem, but it will not stand out, because it's not differentiated."

**Level 3: Is there a competitive moat?**
> "The third lens: Is there a competitive moat that you have thought of when thinking about the solution? Because you can think of any solution, but in today's world, more so than ever before, it is so easy to copy someone else's solution."

> "You launch a feature today, two days later, your competitor also launches. What's the big deal?"

> "Whenever you're thinking of the solution, you have to think also about the competitive advantage of the solution. What is it that will give me the competitive advantage? And these are things which look... which basically showcase the depth of thought. How deeply have you thought about the solution?"

> "So that's the three levels of creativity of solution. So if you have scored low on your solution, look through each of these three levels. I am very sure a lot of you will fail in the first level itself."

> "Your solutions being proposed will fail in the first level. If it crosses the first level, most likely, your second level and the third level has not been thought of."

**My self-assessment:**
- Level 1: I'm confident. Signal directly addresses the trust calibration problem.
- Level 2: I'm reasonably confident. Nobody is doing independent verification + calibration for AI code output inline.
- Level 3: This is my weakest area honestly. The calibration data moat is real but takes time to build. My near-term moat is integration depth (built into Claude) + brand alignment (Anthropic = safety).
